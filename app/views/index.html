Find a framework Version 3

Feature hypothesis

Hypothesis 1 – Find a supplier who is compliant and gives a good deal

User need

I want help finding the best supplier who fits my needs, so that I procure at best value.

Method

Improve the journey to buy goods or service contracts through an improved find a framework journey so that schools can save time looking for approved suppliers who can supply the things they need.

Changes

Since the last find a framework prototype we think that presenting some of the guidance and downloads available in the manual after finding the right framework but before hand-off to the PSBO will help users on their journey.

Research notes

Users often missed the guidance, but when encouraged to explore it, found it valuable. It helped (marginally) users understand better what a Framework was, and to prepare to use them. Users still need support before and after finding a method to buy. PSBO websites still remain the biggest barrier (by a long way) to successful usage of Frameworks (when navigating GOV.UK).

Buying goods or a service contract for your school

Feature hypothesis

Hypothesis 1 – Find a supplier who is compliant and gives a good deal

User need

I want help finding the best supplier who fits my needs, so that I procure at best value.

Method

Improve the journey to buy goods or service contracts through an improved find a framework journey so that schools can save time looking for approved suppliers who can supply the things they need.

Changes

Since the last find a framework prototype we’ve updated the journey and outcomes for frameworks and allowed the user to navigate to guidance on running their own buying process.

Research notes

The prototype encourages the use of frameworks, by highlighting the range of frameworks available, and supporting users to quickly find an appropriate framework. However, users perceptions of frameworks pose a significant barrier - specifically, frameworks are not perceived as the cheapest way to procure goods / services. Additionally, though the prototype saves users time finding a framework, every stage of the user journey from that point remains complex and time-consuming.

Find a supplier for a school Version 3

Feature hypothesis

Hypothesis 1 – Find a supplier who is compliant and gives a good deal
Hypothesis 2 – Supported/managed procurement process

User need

I want to find a supplier who fits my needs, so that I procure at best value.

Method

The design will simplify how buyers find suppliers by letting them post a their requirements and have suppliers respond in to them.

Changes

This version will extend the post a specification route, exploring the service behind it and touching more on how suppliers could repsond and what details the school will get back.

Research notes

The ‘Schools Marketplace’ prototype helps users to access more suppliers, this may help users save money by increasing competition. Like the ‘Create a Specification’ prototype, ‘Schools Marketplace’ saves users time by offering prompts and suggestions, and additionally reduces the time it takes to research suppliers.

Find a supplier for a school Version 2

Feature hypothesis

Hypothesis 1 – Find a supplier who is compliant and gives a good deal
Hypothesis 2 – Supported/managed procurement process

User need

I want help finding the best supplier who fits my needs, so that I procure at best value.

Method

The design will simplify how buyers find suppliers by pointing them to the correct procurement method and assisting them, by pointing them to a framework or helping them post a specificiation for tender.

Changes

Different buying methods are covered on a GOV.UK landing page, rather than having to ask the user about budget. The 'Find a framework' and 'Post a specification' flows are then treated as two seperate tools.

Research notes

The Find a Framework prototype makes deals easier to find and understand. However, this is not a viable solution while the PSBO hand-off remains problematic.

Create a Specification and Post a Specification prototypes were of most utility to inexperienced SBPs, and for ‘uniform’ purchases. Additional functionality (e.g. save and continue) should be considered.
